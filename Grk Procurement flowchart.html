Procurement Life Cycle

Training Guide for Newly Onboarded Employees

1

Need Identification

Responsible: Requesting Department (User / Requester)

  • Identify business / operational need
  • Define detailed specifications / requirements
  • Prepare and submit Purchase Requisition
2

Requisition Review & Approval

Responsible: Approving Manager / Department Head / Finance

  • Verify necessity, completeness & budget availability
  • Approve / Reject / Return for revision
3

Sourcing & Supplier Market Research

Responsible: Procurement Department

  • Identify potential suppliers / vendors
  • Issue RFQ / RFP / RFI
  • Collect and log quotations / proposals
4

Bid / Quote Evaluation & Selection

Responsible: Procurement (+ Technical / Finance if needed)

  • Compare on price, quality, delivery, terms, etc.
  • Shortlist and select preferred supplier
5

Negotiation & Contract Award

Responsible: Procurement + Legal

  • Negotiate commercial & delivery terms
  • Legal review for compliance
  • Finalize and sign contract / agreement
6

Purchase Order Issuance

Responsible: Procurement Department

  • Create and issue official Purchase Order (PO)
  • Send PO to supplier & obtain acknowledgment
7

Goods / Services Receipt & Inspection

Responsible: Receiving / Warehouse + Requesting Dept + QA

  • Receive and physically inspect items/services
  • Verify quantity, quality & specifications
  • Record Goods Receipt Note (GRN)
8

Invoice Receipt & 3-Way Matching

Responsible: Finance / Accounts Payable

  • Receive supplier invoice
  • Match PO + Goods Receipt + Invoice
  • Resolve any discrepancies
9

Payment Processing

Responsible: Finance / Treasury

  • Approve and execute payment
  • Update accounting / financial records
10

Performance Review & Procurement Closeout

Responsible: Procurement + Requesting Department

  • Evaluate supplier performance
  • Document lessons learned & feedback
  • Update vendor records & archive file
